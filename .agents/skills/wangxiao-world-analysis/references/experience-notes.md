# Experience Notes

Use these as reusable macro lessons distilled from the subtitle corpus. They are analytical priors, not immutable conclusions.

## Durable Macro Conclusions

### State Capacity Beats Resource Possession

Resource-rich countries can remain poor when extraction revenue does not become fiscal capacity and public investment. A working state needs decision authority, tax collection, competent bureaucracy, resource revenue entering the treasury, public investment, citizens linked to national development, and both a usable state leviathan and constraints on that leviathan.

### Normal Performance Is a Major Achievement for Small States

For small peripheral countries, "performing normally" can be success: basic order, services, education, tourism, public health, and predictable governance may matter more than dramatic great-power ambition. Analyze what the country can realistically specialize in given geography, scale, and external dependence.

### Infrastructure Buys Time

Infrastructure is not decoration. Electricity, rail, ports, roads, telecom, and water systems create time and option value in an unequal global system. A country with long-horizon public investment can absorb shocks and prepare for the next industrial wave better than a country governed only by short-term market return.

### Tariffs and Sanctions Are Cost-Asymmetry Tests

Do not read tariff numbers symmetrically. Ask which goods are consumer necessities, which inputs have substitutes, which producers still have margin, and which public faces price pain. The side in more urgent need of a deal often reveals itself through timing and venue.

### Technology Must Serve Production and Life

AI, digitalization, and advanced manufacturing should be judged by application, not spectacle. The relevant stack is compute, algorithms, data, engineers, market scale, supply chain, energy, and use cases. A technology becomes strategic when it raises productivity, public service capacity, industrial upgrading, or autonomy.

### Rules Are Power When Enforcement and Interpretation Are Controlled

International order is not only formal law. Power sits in agenda-setting, dispute settlement, standards, lending conditions, sanctions, media framing, and the ability to break rules while forcing others to obey them. Analyze who writes, interprets, and enforces rules.

### Colonial and Dependency Paths Persist

Borders, elite education, language, legal systems, military structures, debt dependence, and export concessions can outlive formal colonialism. Do not explain instability only through current leaders; trace the inherited operating system.

### Value Chain Position Is Often the Real Story

Owning resources or labor is not the same as owning processing, branding, logistics, standards, finance, or final markets. Ask who captures margin and who bears volatility.

## Research Templates

### Country Template

- Map facts: location, neighbors, ports, terrain, resource distribution.
- Society: population, ethnic/religious structure, urbanization, education, labor force.
- State: tax capacity, bureaucracy, military, police, ruling coalition, opposition, local power centers.
- Economy: exports, imports, fiscal revenue, debt, electricity, infrastructure, value-chain position.
- External ties: former colonial power, lenders, military partners, trade partners, international institutions.
- Core judgment: the binding constraint and the plausible path to improvement.

### Conflict Template

- Trigger versus root cause.
- Actor objectives and red lines.
- Cost-bearing structure.
- Time pressure and domestic legitimacy.
- External sponsors and rule/institution constraints.
- Escalation paths, off-ramps, and who benefits from delay.

### Industry Template

- Demand: who needs it and why now.
- Stack: inputs, core tech, energy, logistics, software, standards, talent.
- Substitution: what can be replaced, by whom, and how quickly.
- Value capture: who controls margins, channels, brands, data, finance, and standards.
- State role: policy, procurement, public infrastructure, and strategic autonomy.
