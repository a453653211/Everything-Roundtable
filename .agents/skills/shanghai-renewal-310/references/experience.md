# 310 Renewal Experience Notes

Use this reference when a task requires judgment beyond the core method: buying-plate research, individual estate probability, weak signals, district style, or "underground" information handling.

## Evidence Discipline

Treat information in four layers:

- **Official hard evidence**: 征收决定, compensation plan, budget tables, final planning changes, project approvals, formal公告.
- **Official soft evidence**: leadership wording, district work plans, 十五五/专项规划 language, city-renewal pilots,招商推介, official replies.
- **Field evidence**: repair activity, survey teams, resident征询, neighborhood committee messaging, construction fencing, abnormal vacancy, sales-office narratives.
- **Underground evidence**: local rumors, intermediary claims, resident chat, "heard from inside", unexplained buying interest, contractor gossip.

Use underground evidence only as a search pointer. Never let it become the conclusion unless it is independently supported by official or observable signals.

## Weak-Signal Playbook

Read each weak signal by asking "what mechanism would make this true?"

- **Rumor says it will move**: search for land reserve, budget, plan function change, official reply,征询, nearby major project, or district work plan.
- **Sudden repair**: negative for short-term demolition if high-cost and recent; weakly positive only if repair is minimal, delayed, or inconsistent with building condition.
- **No repair despite obvious decay**: possible weak signal, but could also be budget scarcity, ownership friction, or management neglect.
- **First征询**: meaningful but reversible; the project can still die at money, planning, heritage, stability-risk, or resident-work stages.
- **Public facility planned nearby**: ask whether the planned facility truly needs this parcel now, and whether demographic or service demand still supports it.
- **Metro/road/tunnel nearby**: only strong when station, work shaft, alignment, road red line, or construction approval touches the block.
- **Developer/intermediary excitement**: treat as sentiment, not evidence. Convert it into source checks.
- **District suddenly talks about innovation**: look for pilots, city-renewal units, 三师联创, new安置/房票/活化 mechanisms, and city-level budget support.

## Contrarian Lessons

- **Planning is not destiny**. It is a resource-allocation map; old, ambitious, or expensive planning may sit for years.
- **"合理" is weaker than "合算"**. A road, school, or commercial plan may be logical but still lose to cost, low urgency, or resident friction.
- **Land reserve is a shopping list, not a promise**. It becomes serious when paired with money, responsible unit, timing, and a larger strategic purpose.
- **Budget is necessary, not personal**. A district can have money without touching a specific estate.
- **Historic protection does not mean "never"**. It changes the path: demolition may be unlikely, but活化、人走房留、商业改造、局部腾退 may be possible depending on level and protection requirements.
- **High-rise old buildings are not simply old stock**. Reinforced concrete, height, compensation scale, and demolition difficulty make them hard to move unless a major project must use the site.
- **Recent high-cost repair is a real signal**. It usually lowers short-term demolition odds because public money has just been sunk into the building.
- **A failed改建/原拆原建征询 has a shadow**. It can block or chill re-initiation for years and mark resident work as difficult.

## Building-Type Heuristics

- **旧式里弄 / 简屋 / 不成套**: strong old-renewal candidates when inside center-city pressure areas and not blocked by protection or ownership.
- **小梁薄板 / early public housing**: watch policy-cycle language; likely successor categories after 二级旧里收尾, but exact口径 may split by structure and period.
- **新工房 / 老公房**: not automatically weak; strength depends on age, structure, density, repair difficulty, location, and whether new policy names them.
- **高层 or senior apartments**: usually weak for full demolition; check structural condition, historic status, and unavoidable major infrastructure.
- **高级公寓 / 优秀历史建筑**: full demolition unlikely; study adaptive reuse, hotel/public-space precedents, and protection category.
- **城中村**: read through municipal/suburban redevelopment, land finance,安置, and district platform execution rather than classic old-city logic.

## District And Plate Reading

Do not freeze district stereotypes, but use past behavior as priors:

- **Budget style**: compare initial budget, adjusted budget, and actual execution. Some districts announce big, then under-execute; others announce little and quietly run.
- **Center-city districts**: old-city pressure, historic protection, and resident density dominate. Expect smaller, more complex projects.
- **Suburban districts**: watch城中村, industrial/commercial land, major road/metro projects, and new-town planning more than old-lane logic.
- **Strategic plates**: 滨江、一江一河、北外滩、东外滩、苏河湾、真如、大柏树、五角场、漕河泾、虹桥等 should be judged by whether the specific parcel serves the strategy, not by name proximity.
- **Old commercial visions**: areas with long-standing commercial/CBD colors in plans may still stall if retail reality, fiscal pressure, and resident work do not support implementation.
- **Innovation plates**: city-renewal units and 三师联创 areas matter because they can produce new mechanisms, not because every house inside them will move.

## Buying-Plate Research Routine

Use this routine when the user asks where to buy, whether a plate has upside, or what future infrastructure/renewal signals mean:

1. **Locate the asset precisely**: building, lane, estate, street, district, planning unit.
2. **Read planning hierarchy**: 总体规划 for direction, 单元规划 for plate function, 详细/控规 for parcel reality, 专项规划 for rail/road/tunnel triggers.
3. **Map implementers**: district government, district platform, metro, transport commission, housing bureau, planning resources bureau, education/health/public-service bodies.
4. **Check executable signals**: budget, public notice, project approval, land reserve,征询, construction start, annual work plan.
5. **Score friction**: ownership, protection, recent repair, height/structure, resident difficulty, cross-district coordination.
6. **Separate upside types**: accessibility uplift, public-service uplift, renewal compensation, environment improvement, commercial activation, or pure narrative.
7. **End with scenarios**: base case, upside case, and what evidence would falsify each.

## Questions To Ask During Deep Dives

- Who benefits if this place changes function?
- Who pays?
- Which department would提, and why now?
- Is there a city-level or district-level task that needs this parcel?
- Is the project blocked by planning, heritage, money, engineering, resident work, or ownership?
- Has the district executed similar things before?
- Is the signal recent, repeated, and independently confirmed?
- What would the next official breadcrumb look like?

## Output Guidance

For experience-heavy work, include a small confidence note:

- **High confidence**: official hard evidence plus mechanism alignment.
- **Medium confidence**: multiple official soft signals plus no obvious blocker.
- **Low confidence**: field/underground signals only, or one official signal with major missing links.

Name the missing link directly. Example: "有苗头，但缺预算和责任主体"; "规划有颜色，但缺实施项目"; "传闻很热，但官方链条没有接上".
