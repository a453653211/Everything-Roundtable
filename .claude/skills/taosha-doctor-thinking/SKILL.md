# Taosha Doctor Thinking

## Purpose

Use this skill to analyze macro, market, AI, technology, commodity, and geopolitics-linked events with the distilled reasoning habits found in the 淘沙博士 subtitle corpus. Emphasize first-principles reasoning, source discipline, order-of-magnitude math, and risk framing.

Do not mimic the creator's口癖, profanity, or personality. Do not present outputs as investment advice. When current facts, prices, policies, or people are involved, verify fresh data before concluding.

## Core Workflow

1. Define the object precisely: event, policy, asset, company, industry, or rumor.
2. Separate fact, rumor, market expectation, official plan, analyst forecast, and price reaction.
3. Reduce the issue to first principles:
   - Who pays, who receives, who bears the balance-sheet burden?
   - Does money supply change, or is money only transferred/reclassified?
   - Is the driver demand, supply, inventory, leverage, policy, sentiment, or positioning?
   - Is the constraint physical capacity, cash flow, employment, distribution, regulation, chips/energy/data, or confidence?
4. Quantify before judging. Use denominators, distributions, leverage, duration, turnover, valuation, cost, margin, inventory, and order size. Reject headline totals when net position, concentration, or usable scale matters.
5. Compare expectations with reality. Ask what was already priced in, what is truly incremental, and whether "space to do X" is being confused with "officially plans to do X".
6. Track money and positioning. For market moves, distinguish:
   - fundamentals-driven demand;
   - liquidity-driven repricing;
   - low-float or low-inventory squeezes;
   - policy expectation trades;
   - leverage/forced-covering dynamics.
7. State the conclusion in layers: base judgment, mechanism, strongest evidence, uncertainty, invalidation signal, and risk.
