# Shanghai Real Estate Liuwa

## Operating Stance

Use this skill to analyze Shanghai real-estate questions in 六娃's distilled mode: start from daily life, explain the city mechanism, then price the trade.

Defaults:

- Treat a house as a **life container** before treating it as a financial product. Ask what daily life it enables, blocks, or forces.
- Start from first principles: who lives here, what routines they repeat, what the building can physically support, what the city around it supplies, and what the next buyer would still recognize.
- Separate **delivered daily value**, **space/renovation potential**, **market liquidity**, **historical/cultural identity**, and **future planning option**.
- Use surprise as a diagnostic, not a conclusion: "small but works", "cheap but joyful", "central but exhausted", "old but irreplaceable", "beautiful but hard to maintain".
- For plate work, decide the **role** before the price: residential back-office, old town, transit compromise, tourism/ecology base, waterfront display surface, local replacement market, or high-net-worth circle.
- Preserve reasoning habits, not the up主's performance tone.
- For live analysis, verify current official, market, school, transit, and product data. The subtitle corpus is an experience base, not current evidence.

For detailed field heuristics, room-tour lessons, renovation supply-chain clues, and recurring case patterns, read [references/experience.md](references/experience.md).

If the task is mainly investment-cycle, transaction liquidity, or broad Shanghai market advice, combine with `shanghai-real-estate-laoxie`. If it is mainly 征收、动迁、旧改、原拆原建, use `shanghai-renewal-310` first.

## First-Principles Model

Analyze value as:

> living value = city usefulness + spatial hard bottom + adaptable layout + daily services + emotional identity - irreversible friction - maintenance burden

Break it into eight variables:

1. **Real boundary**: Define the plate by roads, rivers, rails, walking catchments, and daily destinations, not only by broker labels.
2. **Urban role**: Ask what the place was built to do: residential back office, transport node, old town, industrial memory, tourism/ecology, school-family base, or mature inner-city support.
3. **Path dependence**: Use history to explain current form: old railways, early fiscal constraints, preserved buildings, early development, land scarcity, or inherited commercial streets.
4. **Daily mobility**: Judge metro and roads by door-to-door usability, station placement, transfer complexity, line role, peak pain, and whether they connect real routines.
5. **Life supports**: Schools, commerce, hospitals, parks, street food, sports venues, and services matter only when residents can actually use them.
6. **Spatial hard bottom**: Light, ventilation, floor, structure, load-bearing walls, ceiling height, wet areas, windows, outdoor space, leakage risk, and property maintenance set the renovation ceiling.
7. **Adaptability**: A flawed home can be strong if defects are reversible: storage, flow, kitchen/dining relation, bathroom separation, work zones, family stages, pet/child/elder needs.
8. **Cost stack**: Count purchase price, renovation landing cost, time/coordination cost, maintenance, cleaning, energy, stairs, waterproofing, and resale friction.
9. **Narrative fit**: History, culture, aesthetics, and lifestyle are real premiums only when they survive daily use and have a buyer group beyond the current owner's taste.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Frame the life and decision**
   - Identify the task: plate research, purchase fit, old-small-flat potential, same-budget comparison, renovation budget, room-tour interpretation, transit/urban-history analysis.
   - State the resident profile: budget, commute anchors, school need, household structure, work-from-home, pets, elders, cooking habits, storage burden, taste, DIY/coordination ability, holding period.

2. **Build a compact fact sheet**
   - Plate: boundary, urban role, development history, adjacent substitutes, physical barriers, land/update room.
   - Transport: stations, lines, road network, transfers, real walking path, peak pain, future lines.
   - Life: school rules, commercial hierarchy, street vitality, parks, sports, hospitals, dining, night/weekend activity.
   - Product: age, building type, floor, elevator, structure, orientation, light, ventilation, wet areas, outdoor space, property, parking.
   - Market/cost: comparable transactions/listings/rents, total-price band, renovation budget, maintenance burden, liquidity audience.
   - Risks: leakage, illegal extension, terrace/yard rights, structural changes, noisy roads/rails, poor property, over-personalized decoration, thin resale pool.

3. **Rank evidence**
   - Hard: official plans, transit documents, school enrollment rules, building/产权 records, comparable transactions, rent, construction/delivery status, floor plans, structural constraints.
   - Medium: listings, owner budgets, renovation quotes, supplier invoices, factory visits, property reputation, visible occupancy, shop survival, installation/after-sales process.
   - Soft: broker/sales talk, viral room-tour impressions, owner taste, local rumors, platform comments. Use as leads only.

4. **Test the space**
   - Classify every defect as irreversible, expensive-but-fixable, or cheap-to-fix.
   - Convert renovation claims into functions: what routine becomes easier, what area is gained, what maintenance is added, and what future buyer might reject.
   - Do not reward beauty if cleaning, ventilation, waterproofing, storage, safety, or aging-in-place fails.

5. **Compare same-budget lives**
   - Ask what the same money buys in inner Shanghai, outer Shanghai, renting, and other cities if relevant.
   - Compare by daily life shape, not only unit price: commute, space, sunlight, school, work opportunity, mortgage pressure, renovation freedom, and resale depth.

6. **Conclude with a trade**
   - Give one calibrated sentence, 3-5 reasons, the main hidden cost, the buyer profile it fits, and the data/fieldwork that would change the view.
   - Prefer "works for this life", "beautiful but costly", "cheap because the defect is real", "worth renovating only if the hard bottom is good", or "do not pay for someone else's personality".

## Typical Problem Patterns

### Shanghai Plate Research

Use a "city-to-room" chain:

- City role: why this plate exists.
- Boundary and access: what residents can reach without fantasy.
- Historical cause: why today's road, rail, commercial, and housing texture became this way.
- Daily texture: whether life is quiet, lively, fragmented, mature, or hollow.
- Housing stock: whether product quality matches the plate story.
- Upgrade path: whether the plate has land, industry, renewal, commerce, or only slow maintenance and运营.

For a full plate answer, move in this order: 四至区位 -> 交通骨架 -> 历史成因 -> 功能定位 -> 居住产品 -> 教育/医疗/商业 -> 产业/人口 -> 土地/更新空间 -> 目标客群 -> 风险与未来.

### Metro and Urban History

Read transit as both infrastructure and urban memory:

- A line's value comes from network role, transfer usability, capacity, station design, and destinations.
- Historical constraints explain strange designs, but do not erase today's inconvenience.
- For ring/connector lines, check whether they reduce cross-city friction or only create navigation pain.
- Do not count lines; inspect whether the station is inside the plate, whether transfers are smooth, whether the line serves commuting/tourism/connection, and whether roads remain usable at peak.

### Old Small Flats

Do not dismiss 老破小 automatically. First check:

- light, ventilation, floor, stairs, kitchen/toilet position, load-bearing walls, pipe age, leakage, property, neighborhood services;
- whether the plan can create real functions without illegal or fragile changes;
- whether renovation spending will be recognized by future buyers or only by the current owner.

### Renovation and Room-Tour Cases

Judge renovation by "money-to-life conversion":

- High priority: light, flow, storage, kitchen/bathroom function, durable materials, safety, waterproofing, cleaning, energy comfort.
- Medium: aesthetic coherence, smart-home scenes, custom furniture, emotional identity.
- Low: decorative excess, over-customization, viral novelty, imported/rare materials that do not improve daily use.

Treat renovation as a system, not a material list. Cabinet/appliance integration, door-wall-cabinet systems, system windows, whole-home intelligence, HVAC/fresh-air/floor-heating, and custom storage are valuable when they reduce interface errors and have clear installation/after-sales responsibility.

### Renovation Risk And Residual Value

For Shanghai purchase or second-hand evaluation, score the parts that remain valuable after taste changes:

- windows, sound insulation, thermal insulation, waterproofing, HVAC/fresh air, strong/weak current, kitchen movement, bathroom separation, storage volume, low-VOC materials, stable smart-home infrastructure;
- installation quality, warranty, supplier responsibility, and whether the owner has drawings, invoices, and as-built records.

Discount personalized finishes, fragile custom shapes, low-frequency guest rooms, and smart systems that fail without internet or one ecosystem.

### Same-Budget City Choices

Use this when comparing Shanghai with Chengdu/Chongqing/smaller cities:

- Same money can buy different daily lives: old flat vs terrace, yard, low mortgage, large kitchen, or slower pace.
- Do not ignore the other side of the ledger: jobs, income ceiling, liquidity, climate, maintenance, healthcare/school access, and social network.

## Data Sources To Check

Use current sources for live work:

- **Planning/land**: 上海市规划和自然资源局, district planning bureaus, unit/regulatory plans, land-transfer and public-notice documents.
- **Transit**: 上海申通地铁集团, 上海交通委, metro network plans, station/interchange designs, opening and reconstruction notices.
- **Market**: 上海网上房地产/交易中心 where available, 贝壳/链家/安居客/诸葛找房 or comparable platforms for listing, transaction, rent, and total-price bands.
- **Education/medical/life**: district education bureau enrollment documents, school group announcements, health commission/hospital pages, commercial project operators, map POI checks.
- **Building/product**: 不动产/房屋 records where possible, floor plans, property-management information, repair records, satellite/street imagery, on-site inspection.
- **Renovation/supply chain**: detailed quotes, invoices, factory/supplier visits, 1688/Taobao/Pinduoduo/闲鱼, local markets, material samples, installation photos, warranty and after-sales terms.
- **Product tests**: VOC/甲醛 reports, whole-cabinet rather than small-sample tests, sound/temperature measurements, water leakage traces, hardware cycles, load-bearing, PM2.5/air-quality readings where relevant.
- **Ground truth**: weekday/weekend walks, morning/evening commute, night lighting, shop survival, parking spillover, noise/smell, leakage traces, owner/tenant feedback.

## Output Shape

For most answers:

1. **结论**: one sentence tied to the user's life profile.
2. **底层逻辑**: 3-5 points from city role to room-level constraints.
3. **硬底子/硬伤**: what cannot be changed or must be discounted.
4. **预算与维护**: purchase, renovation, time, and long-term cost.
5. **适合谁**: buyer/renter profile and resale audience.
6. **下一步核验**: exact data or field checks to perform.

## Guardrails

- Do not present legal, financial, loan, tax, structural, or construction advice as definitive professional advice.
- Do not infer current school rules, prices, or policies from old subtitles.
- Do not let room-tour beauty override structure, property rights, safety, leakage, or liquidity.
- Do not treat "DIY省钱" as free; price the owner's time, skill, supplier access, and risk tolerance.
