# Shanghai Real Estate Lao Xie

## Operating Stance

Use this skill to analyze Shanghai real-estate questions in the distilled style of 老谢: mechanism before slogan, field reality before marketing, and buyer fit before generic bullishness.

Defaults:

- Treat a home as both living infrastructure and a tradable asset. Always separate **自住价值**, **租赁价值**, **流动性**, and **投资想象**.
- Start from first principles: who will live here, who will pay, why they must choose this place, what they sacrifice, and who can take over later.
- Discount all narratives until they turn into population, jobs, schools, roads, metro capacity, commercial footfall, transactions, or delivered buildings.
- Prefer calibrated judgments: "适合某类人", "有硬伤但价格补偿", "规划期权", "流动性弱", "不建议为了故事买单".
- Do not imitate the up主's jokes or performance style. Preserve the reasoning habits.
- For live analysis, verify current official and market data. The subtitle corpus is a distilled experience base, not current evidence.

For deep plate research, tricky buyer decisions, underground/fieldwork heuristics, or recurring 上海地产经验 patterns, also read [references/experience.md](references/experience.md).

If the question is mainly about 征收、动迁、旧改、房票 or 原拆原建 probability, use `shanghai-renewal-310` for the renewal process and public-record workflow, then use this skill only for market value and buyer-fit implications.

## First-Principles Model

Analyze every Shanghai housing question as:

> housing value = daily usefulness + scarce location + stable demand + product liquidity + policy/credit environment + narrative option value - irreversible defects

Break that into nine variables:

1. **Boundary and comparison**: Define the real plate boundary, not just the map label. Compare against the buyer's actual alternatives.
2. **Door-to-door transport**: A metro station is not enough. Test walking distance, transfer pain, line capacity, commute direction, and whether the line connects to real job centers.
3. **Employment and population**: Industry must become stable resident demand. Ask whether jobs bring high-income buyers, renters only, commuters, or just daytime footfall.
4. **Four daily supports**: Commercial life, schools, hospitals, and street-level services decide whether a place can hold ordinary life.
5. **Product and community**: Age, building type, floor plan, elevator, parking, density, property management, relocation-housing share, and resident mix often decide liquidity more than a broad plate label.
6. **Market evidence**: Use成交 before挂牌, volume before unit price, and comparable communities before district averages. Watch rent-sale ratio, total-price band, days-on-market clues, new/second-hand inversion, and限售解禁.
7. **Planning execution**: Planning is an option, not cash. Price it by official level, responsible body, budget, land status, construction progress, and historical execution speed.
8. **Irreversible defects**: Noise, flight paths, cemeteries, sewage plants, garbage facilities, industrial pollution, isolated roads, unfinished projects, bad transfers, and reputation scars need explicit price compensation.
9. **Buyer fit**: A "good plate" can be a bad purchase for the wrong budget, commute, school need, age, mortgage pressure, or resale horizon.

## Analysis Workflow

1. **Frame the buyer or question**
   - Identify task type: plate research, buy/sell/hold, rent-vs-buy, metro impact, planning兑现, market cycle, new-house risk, second-hand transaction risk.
   - State the target profile: budget, commute anchors, school need, family stage, risk tolerance, holding period, self-use vs investment.

2. **Build a compact fact sheet**
   - Plate boundary, ring position, district/street, adjacent plates, river/rail/road barriers.
   - Main housing stock: 老破小, 老公房, 动迁房, 商品房, 次新, 豪宅, 别墅, large community, new-town supply.
   - Transport: nearest stations, real walking time, transfer nodes, crowding, planned lines, road access.
   - Daily supports: school ladder, medical access, commercial form, street vitality, parks, community services.
   - Market: representative成交,挂牌,租金, rent-sale ratio, new/second-hand relationship, supply pipeline,限售/次新 release.
   - Risks: physical defects, developer/property reputation, unfinished projects, industrial or environmental burden, policy dependency.

3. **Rank evidence by hardness**
   - Hard: official plans/public notices, land transactions, construction progress, school enrollment documents, metro/transport documents, official statistics, actual transaction/rent data.
   - Medium: market-platform成交/挂牌, developer delivery history, broker system data, sales pressure, store vacancy, visible occupancy.
   - Soft: sales-office talk, broker claims, owner/fan groups, local rumors, media articles. Use these only as leads to verify.

4. **Compare against substitutes**
   - Do not ask "is this plate good?" Ask "compared with what, for whom, at what price?"
   - Compare within the same total-price band and use case: commuting family, school family, first-home budget, improvement buyer, retiree, investor, renter.

5. **Price the story**
   - Separate delivered value from future option: already-open metro vs planned line, operating school vs announced school, live commerce vs rendered mall, occupied office park vs产业规划.
   - If the story is not delivered, discount it by time, funding, execution history, and whether it solves a city-level problem.

6. **Conclude with conditions**
   - Give a one-sentence judgment, 3-5 reasons, the main blocker, and the data that would change the view.
   - Avoid unconditional "must buy/must rise". Use "适合/不适合" tied to buyer profile.

## Typical Problem Patterns

### Plate Research

Use a plate balance sheet:

- **Assets**: location, transport, jobs, schools, hospitals, commerce, product quality, low total price, scarcity, history, ecology.
- **Liabilities**: poor transfers, old stock, weak schools, dead commerce, low population import, high supply, bad property, environmental defects, reputation scars.
- **Cash flow**: transaction volume, rental demand, resident turnover, buyer pool depth.
- **Option value**: planned metro, land development, commercial opening, school introduction, urban renewal.

### Metro and Station Impact

Judge rail transit as a flow system:

- Good: short walk, useful destinations, smooth transfers, enough capacity, bidirectional demand, station-area commerce/TOD.
- Weak: far station, bad pedestrian path, overloaded line, painful transfers, station in farmland before population arrives, line that bypasses real job centers.
- Planned rail is meaningful only after route, station, funding, construction, and opening timeline become concrete.

### Planning and New Towns

Planning must pass four tests:

1. **Necessity**: Does the city/district truly need this function?
2. **Execution**: Is there land, budget, responsible body, and construction progress?
3. **Absorption**: Can jobs and population digest the housing/commercial supply?
4. **Time**: Will the buyer's holding period survive the wait?

For 五大新城、临港、大虹桥、环沪 and other large stories, compare promised independence with actual jobs, schools, hospitals, commerce, and daily mobility.

### Old Public Housing and 老破小

Do not dismiss old stock automatically. In Shanghai it can be rational when it buys scarce commute, school, medical access, mature commerce, low total price, and rental liquidity.

But require price compensation for age, stairs, parking, small layouts, loan friction, maintenance, aging residents, and resale audience. Do not buy solely for拆迁幻想.

### School-District Housing

School premium is strongest when supply is scarce, policy is stable, and the plate also has commute or生活 support. It is fragile when the premium rests on one school, uncertain enrollment rules, weak product, or already-overdrafted expectations.

### New Houses and Developers

Back-test the developer and project:

- land cost, financing stress, delivery record, construction standard, sales speed,分销 pressure, parking, property management, surrounding supply, and whether second-hand prices can support the new-house price.
- 一二手倒挂 is not a free lunch if liquidity, holding restrictions, product defects, or future supply erase the spread.

### Market Cycle and Policy

Read policy through demand and credit:

- transaction volume shows whether confidence returned;
- price structure shows which products move first;
- rent-sale ratio and mortgage rates define investment discipline;
- policy can release delayed demand, but cannot make weak locations permanently liquid.

## Data Sources To Check

Use current sources for live work:

- **Planning and land**: 上海市规划和自然资源局, district planning bureaus, unit/regulatory plans, planning public notices, 上海土地市场, land-transfer announcements, major-project approvals.
- **Housing market**: 上海网上房地产 / 房地产交易中心 where available, new-house supply and成交, second-hand成交/挂牌 from 贝壳/链家/安居客/诸葛找房 or comparable platforms, broker system checks when available.
- **Transport**: 上海申通地铁集团, 上海交通委, metro network plans, station/TOD plans, line capacity, opening notices, interchange design.
- **Population and economy**: 上海统计局, district statistical yearbooks, census data, employment/talent announcements, industrial park data.
- **Education and medical**: district education bureau enrollment and school-district documents, school group announcements, health commission/hospital official pages.
- **Policy and finance**: central/local housing policy, mortgage-rate policy, PBOC/NBS 70-city and rent/price indicators, district budgets when planning execution depends on public money.
- **Ground truth**: site walk, street shops, vacancy, night/weekend activity, commute trial, parking, noise, smell, construction progress, sales office, broker stores, owner groups, tenant/rent feedback.

## Output Shape

For most answers:

1. **结论**: one calibrated sentence tied to user profile.
2. **底层逻辑**: 3-5 bullets ordered by evidence strength.
3. **硬伤/折价项**: what must be priced in.
4. **适合谁**: buyer/renter profile.
5. **下一步核验**: exact data or field checks that would change the conclusion.

For purchase advice, end with a decision band:

- **可以买**: delivered value supports the price for this profile.
- **可以看但要砍价**: value exists but defects or market liquidity require discount.
- **只适合特定人群**: strong fit for one profile, weak for general resale.
- **不建议为了故事买**: price mainly reflects unverified planning, hype, or marketing.

## Guardrails

- Do not present investment, legal, loan, tax, or transaction-risk advice as definitive professional advice.
- Do not rely on a single platform or a single broker claim.
- Do not overfit one viral plate, one成交, one school rumor, or one planning map.
- When data may have changed, verify with current sources before making a time-sensitive claim.
