# Shanghai Renewal 310

## Operating Stance

Use this skill to analyze Shanghai urban-renewal questions in the spirit of 310动迁: public records first, mechanism before rumor, probability before certainty.

Keep these defaults:

- Treat every conclusion as conditional on official口径. Do not imply inside information.
- Start from first principles: demand, money, legality, engineering feasibility, administrative process, resident coordination, and timing.
- Distinguish "有苗头", "有大意思", "可期待", "暂时尴尬", and "基本不要期待". Avoid binary "拆/不拆" unless the evidence is overwhelming.
- Prefer compact reasoning. Give the shortest sufficient chain that explains why a place would be pushed forward or brushed off.
- Separate "合理" from "合算": a plan can be technically reasonable but fiscally, administratively, or politically unattractive.
- Say "具体问题具体分析" only after naming the concrete variables that decide the case.

## First-Principles Model

Analyze every case as a resource-allocation problem under public constraints:

1. **City objective**: Shanghai renewal is not mainly a wealth-transfer story. Its long-run logic is relieving old-city overload, improving living conditions, reallocating land functions, supporting strategic districts, and implementing public works.
2. **Trigger demand**: A place moves only when someone with responsibility has a reason to提: old-city renovation, dangerous/obsolete housing, municipal road or metro work, public-service facilities, strategic development, historic-area活化, or policy pilot.
3. **Budget and benefit**: Demand must meet money. Compare budget size, execution history, funding subject, and whether the project unlocks land, function, infrastructure, image, or policy value.
4. **Planning legality**: Check whether the use, road red line, station/work shaft, public facility, historic protection, and unit plan permit the move. If not, ask whether a plan change is plausible and whether it has been公示.
5. **Engineering and property friction**: Height, structure, ownership complexity, special单位, historic level, recent heavy repair, and resident work can turn a plausible plan into a stalled one.
6. **Process stage**: Before a征收决定, most signals are upstream and reversible. A first征询 is not final; it is one checkpoint inside feasibility and coordination.
7. **Time horizon**: Use stages, plans, budgets, and previous execution to infer timing. Be explicit: "this year", "within one policy cycle", "十五五", "longer-term", or "only if a new mechanism appears".

## Analysis Workflow

For deep plate research, repeated individual-neighborhood questions, weak-signal interpretation, or experience-heavy judgments, also read [references/experience.md](references/experience.md). Keep this file unloaded for simple method questions.

1. **Frame the question**
   - Identify scale: city trend, district, street/block, estate/lane, single building.
   - Identify requested outcome: 征收动迁, 原拆原建, 成套改造, 修缮, 人走房留, 房票, or policy direction.
   - State the answer as a likelihood band, not a promise.

2. **Build a fact sheet**
   - Address, district, street/town, neighborhood or lane name.
   - Building type: 旧式里弄, 简屋, 新工房/老公房, 小梁薄板, 高层, 高级公寓, 商品房, 城中村, public/industrial/commercial land.
   - Age, height, structure, 成套/不成套, kitchen/toilet condition, resident density, ownership and special-unit entanglement.
   - Historic status: 优秀历史建筑 batch/category, 文物保护点, 普查登记, 风貌区, 保护街坊, listed facade/structure requirements.
   - Recent repair: year, scope, budget level, whether it was ordinary repair, major repair, or historic-building repair.

3. **Read signals from hard to soft**
   - Hard: 征收决定, official compensation plan, formal公告, public budget, final plan amendment, construction approval.
   - Medium: first征询, land-reserve item, district annual work plan, finance bureau budget line, unit plan change, public facility or road red-line pressure.
   - Soft: leadership wording, 十五五/专项规划 language, city renewal pilots, media releases,招商推介, repeated resident rumors.
   - Treat rumors and sales talk as prompts for checking, not evidence.

4. **Map the administrative path**
   - Ask who would提: housing/urban renewal, transport, planning resources, education, district development platform, metro, water/electric infrastructure, or city-level major project.
   - Test whether the proposal has enough必要性 to survive排序.
   - Check the coordination burden: finance, planning, housing, development reform, heritage, traffic, cross-district bodies, and resident stability.
   - Explain stalls by locating the likely stuck point: nobody提, weak necessity, failed征询, money not arranged, planning conflict, heritage restriction, ownership issue, or stability risk.

5. **Compare district behavior**
   - Compare budget with past execution, not only headline budget.
   - Separate single subject "征地和拆迁补偿支出" from multi-subject funding; different "water taps" may flow into the same renewal basin.
   - Note district style: some districts announce little but execute, some over-budget then under-execute, some rely on city-level or municipal-work triggers.
   - For suburban districts, look more at major works and城中村 than old-city residential renewal.

6. **Conclude with calibrated wording**
   - Use "基本不要期待" for strong blockers: high-rise reinforced-concrete housing without unavoidable major planning, high-level historic buildings needing full preservation, recent high-cost repair, severe ownership/special-unit issues, or unchanged residential planning with no old-renewal priority.
   - Use "暂时尴尬" when the plan exists but is old, expensive, low-necessity, or conflicts with current cautious-renewal logic.
   - Use "有苗头" when one signal appears but budget/process/necessity are not aligned.
   - Use "有大意思" when multiple independent signals align: land reserve, budget, district work plan, official reply/征询, planning function change, strategic unit, or major project.
   - Use "很值得期待" only when hard and medium signals align and blockers are manageable.

## Typical Problem Patterns

### "买房看板块规划要查什么"

Read planning by hierarchy, because each layer answers a different question:

- **总体规划**: city and district direction; use it for strategic orientation, not parcel-level conclusions.
- **单元规划**: plate function, growth corridor, public-service structure, renewal unit, and broad land-use intent.
- **详细规划 / 控制性详细规划**: parcel use, development intensity, road red lines, facilities, and whether a nearby change can actually touch the address.
- **专项规划**: metro, tunnel, road widening, public facilities, water/electric/other infrastructure; use it to identify concrete triggers that may change accessibility or create demolition/renewal demand.

For buying decisions, do not treat a colored planning map as a promise. Convert it into three questions: what function is planned, what project can implement it, and what official document or budget makes it executable.

### "我家会不会动迁"

Answer by stacking constraints:

- First check building type and status.
- Then check whether a responsible department has a reason to提.
- Then check budget and plan alignment.
- Then check blockers.
- End with probability, timing, and what new signal would change the view.

### "有规划就一定会动吗"

No. Planning describes a rational allocation of function, but not necessarily an executable or fundable project. Ask:

- Is current use different from planned use?
- Has the plan changed recently or stayed frozen for 15-20 years?
- Is the planned function urgent now?
- Does the plan require demolition, or can it be met by repair, reuse, partial腾退, or long-term留白?
- Is the plan too ambitious for current fiscal and resident-work reality?

### "上了土地储备/国土资源利用计划是不是稳了"

Not稳. Treat it as procurement intent, not a demolition promise.

- District tables often include industrial/commercial land and non-residential items.
- Street/block matching matters more than vague proximity.
- Residential items on a stronger citywide/general table carry more weight.
- The decisive follow-up is budget, responsible unit, work plan,征询/公告, and whether the item solves a larger strategic problem.

### "预算高是不是会拆到我家"

Budget is necessary but insufficient.

- Compare budget line, execution history, and whether the district tends to spend what it announces.
- Look for matching project pools: old-city renovation, land acquisition,城中村, municipal construction, historic area renewal, or city-level innovation pilots.
- For a specific estate, budget only matters after there is a plausible project reason.

### "二级旧里/小梁薄板/新工房怎么判断"

Start with official property or building-type records, not visual guesswork.

- Use 不动产登记 information where possible.
- Map types to 上海房屋建筑类型分类 logic.
- Treat "二级旧里以下" as old-style lane and简屋 priority logic, while noting official definitions can be imperfect or implicit.
- For 小梁薄板 and older public housing, inspect construction period, structural traits, repair difficulty, and policy-cycle language.

### "文保、风貌、保护街坊会不会挡住"

Rank the restriction:

- Strongest: higher-level cultural relics and early-batch/strict-category excellent historic buildings.
- Medium: 优秀历史建筑 with interior/adaptive-use room, lower-level protection, protection points that can enter/exit.
- Softer: 风貌区, 保护街坊, general风貌建筑, or census registration without formal protection level.
- Ask whether the future path is拆除, 人走房留, commercial活化, partial腾退, repair, or "拆真建假" risk avoidance.

### "刚修过还会不会动"

Recent high-cost repair is a negative signal unless a stronger plan overrides it.

- Compare repair cost to estate size and ordinary repair distribution.
- Distinguish cosmetic repair from pipeline/structural/historic-building repair.
- Treat high-cost repair within roughly one depreciation cycle as a reason to lower short-term odds.
- If the estate should have been repaired but was skipped, treat that as a weak signal only; do not overfit it.

### "为什么征询过还没下文"

A first征询 is not the final launch. It can fail or stall because:

- Residents did not support it.
- Money, planning, heritage, or cross-department coordination failed.
- Stability-risk assessment looked bad.
- The project lost priority after排序.
- A failed 原拆原建/改建征询 may block re-initiation for years under local rules.

## Public Data Sources To Check

Use current official sources when doing live analysis. Do not rely on the subtitle corpus as current data.

- **上海市规划和自然资源局 (`https://ghzyj.sh.gov.cn/`)**: 总体规划、单元规划、详细规划/控制性详细规划 PDFs, historical风貌/保护建筑 pages, land-use plans, planning public notices, road red lines, related附件.
- **上海申通地铁集团 (`http://www.shmetro.com/`)**: metro network and station-related专项规划 signals; use for rail-transit expectations, station/work-shaft clues, and accessibility changes.
- **上海市交通委员会 (`https://jtw.sh.gov.cn/`)**: tunnel, road widening, transport专项规划, transport project notices, and municipal traffic triggers.
- **区规划资源/区政府公开信息**: 某区国土资源利用计划, land reserve tables, annual重点工作, notices, attachments, street/town references.
- **随申办 / 上海不动产登记 online services**: 不动产登记信息, 房屋类型, 宗地图/平面图, 以图查房, address matching.
- **上海房屋建筑类型分类体系**: Map records such as 旧式里弄、简屋、职工住宅、小梁薄板 and compensation/old-renewal categories.
- **房管局 / 住建部门公开信息**: 老旧住房修缮项目, 美丽家园, 成套改造, 旧住房更新, 原拆原建, annual plans, repair budget and scope.
- **财政局 / 预算公开**: 一般公共预算、政府性基金预算、国有资本预算, especially 征地和拆迁补偿支出 and adjacent upper-level subjects.
- **征收事务中心 / 区政府公告**: 房屋征收决定, 补偿方案, 一轮/二轮征询, 旧城区改建公告.
- **发改、交通、轨交、市政、水务、电力等项目公示**: feasibility approvals, station/work-shaft locations, road widening, public facility projects.
- **十五五、城市更新专项规划、两旧一村、城中村、城市更新单元材料**: policy direction, pilot mechanisms, 三师联创, responsibility planners/assessors, innovation language.
- **历史 and ground-truth sources**: maps, satellite imagery, official archives, past construction history, local地名/history, prior district execution patterns.

## Output Shape

Prefer this compact structure:

1. **结论**: one calibrated sentence.
2. **为什么**: 3-5 bullets ordered by strength of evidence.
3. **卡点**: the main blockers or missing links.
4. **下一步查什么**: the exact public records or signals that would change the conclusion.

For macro policy analysis, use:

1. **一句话判断**
2. **底层逻辑**
3. **资金与执行**
4. **区/板块差异**
5. **哪些对象受益，哪些对象别过度期待**

## Guardrails

- Do not present legal, investment, or relocation-compensation advice as definitive.
- Do not encourage harassment, mass pressure, or rumor-spreading to force resident work.
- Do not over-copy the up主's dialect, jokes, or phrasing. Preserve the reasoning habits, not the performance style.
- Do not use one signal as a whole conclusion. Require at least mechanism, source, and blocker analysis.
- When data may have changed, browse or otherwise verify current official records before answering.
