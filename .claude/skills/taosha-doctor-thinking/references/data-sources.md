# Data Sources

Use fresh primary sources first. The subtitle corpus repeatedly relies on official data, company disclosures, market microstructure, sell-side research, and reputable financial media. Treat social-media posts and "小作文" as leads, not evidence.

## Macro And Policy

- China: National Bureau of Statistics, People's Bank of China, Ministry of Finance, CSRC, NDRC, Ministry of Commerce, Ministry of Industry and Information Technology, State Council/major meeting releases, local-government notices.
- US/global: Federal Reserve/FOMC statements, Beige Book, BLS CPI/nonfarm/unemployment data, BEA GDP/PCE data, US Treasury releases, CME FedWatch, ADP employment data.
- Core indicators: CPI, core CPI, PPI, PMI, GDP, GDP deflator, M0/M1/M2, social financing, LPR, nonfarm payrolls, unemployment, participation rate, bond yields, yield-curve shape.

## Markets

- Equity: exchange announcements, turnover, market cap, sector performance, ETF flows/premiums, margin financing, northbound/southbound/foreign flows where available.
- Bonds: China interbank bond market, Treasury yields, government-bond futures, curve slope and duration risk.
- Commodities: COMEX, London market inventory/price references, domestic exchange inventory, spot-futures spreads, deliverable inventory, ETF moves.
- Useful checks: turnover relative to free float, leverage embedded in futures/ETFs, forced-covering risk, and whether price action is happening in a thin market.

## Company And Industry

- Company filings: annual reports, interim/quarterly reports, exchange announcements, inquiry letters, risk warnings, guidance, buybacks/reductions, financing plans.
- Banks and household finance: bank annual reports and retail deposit distributions; use examples like CMB only as evidence for concentration, not as a universal constant.
- AI/semiconductors: model technical reports, papers, API pricing pages, cloud rental/capex disclosures, earnings calls from Nvidia/Microsoft/OpenAI ecosystem firms, SIA/SEMI/IDC-style industry statistics when primary figures are unavailable.
- Autos/consumer electronics/robotics: sales, deliveries, order books, component supply, subsidy rules, price cuts, inventory, and margin impact.

## Media And Sell-Side

Commonly useful secondary sources:

- Bloomberg, Reuters, Wall Street Journal, New York Times, CCTV, Xinhua.
- Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, JPMorgan, Nomura and other sell-side research.

Use these for leads, consensus, and market framing. Verify the original wording because the corpus repeatedly distinguishes "an economist says there is room for issuance" from "the state plans issuance."

## Collection Discipline

For each important claim, record:

- source type: official, filing, market data, sell-side, media, social;
- publication time and data period;
- exact metric and denominator;
- whether the number is headline, net, component, distribution, or revised data;
- what would make the source misleading.

When data conflicts, prefer primary-source mechanics over narrative authority. If no primary source is available, mark the conclusion as provisional.
